<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li class="has-medium-font-size"><strong>Appraisal Fee:</strong> <br><br>This fee covers the cost of a professional appraisal, which is a necessary step in the mortgage process. <br><br>The appraisal ensures that the lender is not providing a loan for more than the property's worth. <br><br>This fee is paid directly to the appraisal company and is usually required to be paid upfront.<br></li>

<li class="has-medium-font-size"><strong>Home Inspection Fee:</strong> <br><br>The home inspection is a critical step for the buyer to understand the condition of the property. <br><br>This fee is paid to a home inspector, who assesses the property for any potential issues. Like the appraisal fee, this is usually an upfront cost.</li>
</ol>

<p>It's important to note that these fees are paid outside of escrow and are typically non-refundable. This is because:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="has-medium-font-size"><strong>Immediate Service Payment:</strong> <br><br>These fees cover services that are rendered immediately after going into contract. <br><br>Once the appraisal and inspection are completed, the professionals involved have provided their services and must be compensated.<br></li>

</ul>
