This form requires tenants to provide detailed pet information, promoting transparency and ensuring responsible pet ownership in rental properties.</p>

<h2>Nevada's Fair Housing Law for Service Animals</h2>

<p><a href="https://www.leg.state.nv.us/nrs/nrs-118.html#NRS118Sec105">Nevada's NRS 118.105</a> mandates that landlords cannot refuse to rent a dwelling to a person with a disability on the grounds that the tenant will reside with a service animal. </p>

<p>This law, part of <a href="https://www.leg.state.nv.us/nrs/nrs-118a.html">Chapter 118A</a>, ensures that people with disabilities are granted fair access to housing, with the provision that their service animals are an essential part of their living arrangement. </p>

<p>However, landlords are within their rights to request proof that the animal is indeed a service, support, or assistance animal, which can typically be substantiated by a statement from a healthcare provider.</p>
